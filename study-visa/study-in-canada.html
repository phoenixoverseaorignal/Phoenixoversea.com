How to get Canada Study Visa?

You have to fulfill the following eligibility criteria to get the Canada Student Visa:

Educational Qualification

The minimum eligibility requisite is you have to obtain a Graduate Degree with a minimum of 50% from a University recognized by UGC or AICTE

Back Year

You have to complete the degree within the specified timeline and do not have any year gap.

  • B.Com is a degree program of 36 months. You must complete it within 36 months and no further
  • Engineering is a degree program of 48 months. You must complete it within 48 months and no further.
Backlogs

You must not have backlogs of more than 10 in your period of graduation.

CANADIAN EDUCATION SYSTEM
  • Universities and higher education institutions in Canada offer Degree, Diploma, Certificate, and Advanced Diploma Programs across the nation
  • Canada offers you top-notch education at reasonable costs and with early enrollment scholarships
  • You can access the privilege to work in Canada for 1 to 3 years through Post-study work rights and also work part-time while studying

If you observe the latest trends, you will find that majority of the overseas students choose Canada as their favored destination for pursuing higher education overseas.

INTAKES/ADMISSION TIME IN CANADA

  • Universities and Colleges in Canada offer 3 intakes/ Admission windows:
  • Intake /Admission window One - Fall Semester, a trendy intake which begins in September
  • Intake /Admission window Two - Winter Semester which begins in January
  • Intake /Admission window Three - Summer Semester typically begins from April or May and is available for limited institutions and courses.

Learner work Authorization

Overseas learners seeking work authorization must be above eighteen years. They are permitted to work away from campus or within the campus for 20 hours weekly during academic sessions and full-time during study breaks. This includes summer or wintry weather vacations. They do not require a Work Permit for this.

STUDY VISA PREREQUISITES

You will usually need the following for your Canada Study Visa submission:

  1. Legal Passport
  2. Attested photocopy of your Degree, Class 12 and 10 Certificates
  3. Two academic references
  4. Two employer references
  5. Statement of Purpose
  6. Credentials of your co-curricular accomplishments
  7. Letter of Acceptance from an educational institution in Canada
  8. Confirmation of payment of fees
  9. Confirmation of monetary resources
  10. Photos of Passport size
  11. Request form for Study Visa
  12. Confirmation of proficiency in the English language

How to apply Canada Study Visa?

The Canada Study Visa Process is as follows:

Step 1: Explore Your Options

Prior to short-listing the institutions or universities, you must first decide your study program, the destination in Canada, overall expenditures, diverse prospects, and other factors.

Step 2: Choose Universities

The next step involves identifying ten institutions and courses in line with your career goals and preferred Canadian location.

Step 3: Get ready for Entrance Exams

In the next stage, you must get ready for standardized tests like IELTS, TOEFL, GMAT, or GRE depending on the specifications of the institutions and Universities

Step 4: Financial support for your studies

You must make sure that you possess the finances needed for your complete period of studies in Canada. It includes your local journey, accommodation, food, and various costs.

Step 5: Submit requests to Universities

You must directly get in touch with each University for admission criteria. The specifications differ from one University to another. Complete requests much prior to submission deadlines and submit them.

Step 6: Validate Admission

Once you get Letters of Admission from some Universities, select a University that is your preferred choice. The next stage is a payment of a deposit to the University which is non-refundable and confirms your acceptance for study in Canada.

Step 7: Get Student Visa

Once you receive the authentication for admission, you can file a request for a Study Visa.

Frequently Asked Questions(FAQs)

On average in Canada, university tuition costs around: $36,100 per year for international undergraduate students (Statistics Canada, 2022) $21,100 per year for international graduate students (Statistics Canada, 2022).

This is one of the reasons so many young people choose to live here. Students should budget for about 10000 to 12000 Canadian dollars per year to cover cost of living expenses such as residence or rent, meal plan or groceries, cell phone plans and spending money.

There are no tuition-free universities in Canada, either for international or domestic students. However, some universities are backed by fully-funded scholarships that will fund students' complete education.
