What is a Canadian ? Comprehensive Ranking System?

The Comprehensive Ranking System or CRS points calculator is a system adopted by the Canadian Government for evaluation, allocation of scores, and offering ranks. It is for the profiles of the candidates in the pool of Federal Express Entry.

Your score under the CRS calculator will be determined by the answers that you have offered in your profile in Express Entry. The overall score that you get under CRS will decide if you are qualified for receiving Permanent Resident status. So this is a tool that decided your eligibility for immigrating to Canada.

The point calculator under CRS has been modified for assessing the scores of the candidates under the skilled worker immigration category. Applicants are now required to secure at least 67 points to qualify for submitting an application for skilled worker immigration. The assessment is based on diverse factors like age, work experience, language proficiency, education, etc.

A minimum score under CRS for entering the candidates’ pool in Express Entry does not exist. The cut-off score under CRS keeps varying from one draw to other. It can decline or rise based on numerous factors.

The least score required to receive the invitation is not announced by Immigration, Refugees, and Citizenship Canada prior to any selection draw. So, the higher you score under the CRS points calculator; the better your prospects of getting the ITA.

How CRS Points Calculator Works?

To calculate CRS score for candidates, Express Entry utilizes the credentials that the candidates provide while creating their profiles in its pool. The factors that decide the selection include among others their age, education, language skills, etc.

Canadian immigration points calculator offers points to profiles of candidates in the Express Entry pool depending on the below factors:
  • Human Capital / Core factors
  • Accompanying spouse or common-law partner factors
  • Transferability of Skill Factors
  • Other factors – Provincial nomination, eligible prearranged job offer, education in Canada, a family member in Canada, or French language skills
  • Candidates in the Express Entry pool have a maximum of 1200 points available under the Canadian PR points calculator.
The candidates not having an accompanying spouse or common-law partner have:
  • Up to 500 points that can be secured for Human Capital /Core factors
  • Up to 100 points that can be secured for the Transferability of the skills factor
  • Up to 600 points that can be secured for nomination from a province or
  • Up to 200 points that can be secured for an eligible approved job offer
  • Up to 30 points that can be secured for having studied in Canada
  • Up to 15 points that can be secured for a family in Canada
  • Up to 30 points that can be secured for having studied in Canada
The candidates having an accompanying spouse or common-law partner have:
  • Up to 460 points that can be secured for the primary applicant’s Human Capital /Core factors
  • Up to 40 points that can be secured for Human Capital /Core factors of the accompanying spouse or common-law partner
  • Up to 600 points that can be secured for nomination from a province or
  • Up to 200 points that can be secured for an eligible approved job offer
  • Up to 30 points that can be secured for having studied in Canada
  • Up to 50 points that can be secured for ability in the French language and English language
  • Up to 15 points that can be secured for a family in Canada (one family member of the primary applicant and the accompanying spouse or common-law partner is considered)

What are the How to better your CRS Score?

The selection draws under Express Entry are conducted regularly normally once in 14 days. Thus, you always have the opportunity to enhance your score in CRS prior to applying for a succeeding Express Entry draw. Many options exist for increasing your CRS score if you are unable to secure the cut-off CRS needed for the specific draw.

By one or more measures, you can enhance your score in the CRS points calculator and achieve the points needed for receiving the Invitation to Apply – ITA for PR Visa in the imminent draws.

Many factors could be responsible for one’s lower score under the Canadian PR score calculator after entry into the Express Entry candidates’ pool. You can determine the reasons for losing points by comparing the summary of maximum points in CRS over your score.

If your score in CRS is low, it can be truly disheartening. However, you must never give up and work on your profile for improving your ranking after submission in the Express Entry pool. Several candidates are able to better their scores in CRS after obtaining extra work experience, reappearing for their language exam, obtaining a job offer in Canada, or opting for study in Canada. There are several opportunities to improve your ranking.

Some of the ways for increasing your score in CRS are as follows:

Increasing your score on language test

By securing higher bands in language tests like IELTS you can increase your CRS score. For example, if you get a 9 CLB Level in the language test, you can receive up to 136 points towards your CRS score. Likewise, results from tests for the French language can offer you up to 72 points.

Provincial Nominee Program

You can receive 600 additional points towards your Express Entry CRS score if you get a nomination from a Canadian province.

Obtain a job offer Approved by LMIA

You can receive 200 additional points towards your Express Entry CRS score if you get an offer of a job from an employer in Canada. The job offer has to be approved through the Labor Market Impact Assessment process.

Study in Canada

You can receive 200 additional points towards your Express Entry CRS score if you get a recognized Degree or Diploma in Canada.

Including Spouse / Common-Law Partner in the application Partner in the application

You will get additional points for including your Spouse / Common-Law Partner in the application. You can obtain extra points for three factors. 20 points will be offered to you for the language proficiency of your Spouse / Common-Law Partner. 10 points will be offered for work experience in Canada and 10 points for studies in Canada. So, you can get up to 40 extra CRS points.

Canadian Work Experience

If you possess less than 3 years of work experience full-time and are still employed, you can receive up to 150 points toward your score in CRS under Canada PR points calculator.

What score is needed for PNPs under CRS?

The eligibility criteria for every PNP are unique as decided by the respective provinces. A few of the PNPs seek potential candidates in the Express Entry System also. Most of the PNPs have a unique system of calculation of points. A few PNPs can also consider the CRS score of the candidate along with other factors for deciding the ITA for nomination.

The required CRS scores are variable from one draw to other. A few of the provinces specify the least CRS score needed to be considered for the nomination. For instance, the HCP stream of Ontario does not offer ITAs to candidates with scores less than 400 in CRS. Express Entry Alberta requires a minimum of 300 CRS scores.
