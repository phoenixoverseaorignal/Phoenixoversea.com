What is the Manitoba Provincial Nominee Program?

Manitoba Provincial Nominee Program or Manitoba PNP is the Provincial Nominee Program of Manitoba Province. It aims to select skilled immigrant workers. They are hired to fulfill the labor market and financial demands of the Province. Eligible overseas candidates can apply to the MPNP for receiving Canada Permanent Resident Visa.

What are the chief immigration pathways under Manitoba PNP?

The important immigration pathways under the Manitoba Provincial Nominee Program are:
  • Skilled Worker Category
  • Overseas Skilled Worker Category
  • Manitoba Skilled Worker Category
  • Business Investor Category
  • Overseas Education Category
The immigration sub-categories under the category Overseas Skilled Worker are:
  • Express Entry Manitoba Pathway
  • Human Capital Pathway
The immigration sub-categories under the category Manitoba Skilled Worker are:
  • Work Experience Manitoba Pathway
  • Direct Recruitment Employer Pathway
The immigration sub-categories under the category Overseas Education are:
  • Graduate Internship Program
  • Career Employment Program
  • Entrepreneur Pilot Overseas Student

What are the Eligibility Criteria for Manitoba PNP?

You are required to fulfill various requirements in order to fulfill the Manitoba PNP eligibility. The required eligibility criteria vary based on the chosen MPNP immigration category and sub-category.

What are the Required Documents for Manitoba PNP? CRS Score?

The documents needed for Manitoba Provincial Nominee Program are variable. This will depend on the immigration pathway that you have chosen.

However, there are a few common documents that are needed for MPNP. These include:

  • Proof of identity
  • Authentic Passport
  • ECA - Educational Credential Assessment Report
  • Test results for proficiency in a language, for example, IELTS
  • Certificate of Marriage if being accompanied by your spouse
  • Information Certificate of Dependent Children if you possess a dependent kid
  • Medical records
  • Certificate of Police clearance
  • Evidence of funds
  • Plan for Settlement
  • Employment Letter, if required
  • Evidence of past work experience
  • Evidence of connection, if required

How to file an application for Manitoba PNP?

Method 1

Step 1: You must confirm if you are qualified to file an application for Canada Express Entry

Step 2: You can create an online profile in Express Entry if you fulfill the criteria

Step 3: You have to then file the required documents

Step 4: You have to then obtain the Job Seeker Validation Code and Express Entry Registration Code

Step 5: Later you have to create a new profile in the official portal of Manitoba PNP

Step 6: Then you have to connect both the profiles

Step 7: Your application will then be received in the Express Entry pool of candidates

Step 8: Prospective applicants are viewed by Manitoba

Step 9: Candidates who are assessed to be suitable will be offered the Notification of Interest

Step 10: The NOI will be sent to you via the Express Entry portal

Step 11: You have to file the Expression of Interest or EOI after getting the NOI

Step 12: You have to wait to receive the nomination from MPNP

Step 13: Your CRS score will enhance by 600 points if you receive the Provincial Nomination

Step 14:You will mostly receive the Invitation to Apply for Canada PR Visa with such a high CRS score

Step 15:You have to file an application for PR Visa with the Immigration, Refugees, and Citizenship Canada

Step 16: The full application, extra documents, and fees have to submitted within sixty days

Step 17:You must immigrate to Manitoba within the timeline specified after receiving the PR Visa

Method 2

Step 1: You must confirm the eligibility criteria of various immigration categories and sub-categories under Manitoba PNP

Step 2: You can file the Expression Of Interest for any of the categories for which you are qualified

Step 3: You will be entered into a pool of eligible candidates

Step 4: According to the MPNP EOI Ranking Grid for Points, your profile will be evaluated

Step 5: You will be offered a score as per the EOI Ranking

Step 6: You will receive the Letter of Advice to Apply if you are among the candidates scoring the highest in EOIs

Step 7:You have to file the full application within sixty days to MPNP after receiving the LAA

Step 8: You have to wait to receive the nomination from MPNP

Step 9: You have to file an application for PR Visa with the Immigration, Refugees, and Citizenship Canada

Step 10: The full application, extra documents, and fees have to be submitted within sixty days of receiving the nomination

Step 11: You must notify Manitoba Provincial Nominee Program once you receive the Permanent Resident status

Is it effortless to get Manitoba PNP? Manitoba PNP?

Getting the nomination from MPNP is easy if you fulfill the eligibility criteria for the chosen Manitoba immigration category. The eligibility criteria vary based on the immigration stream. However, there are a few common eligibility criteria as well. These include:

  • You have to be capable of submitting the specified documents
  • The details that you submit in your profile have to correspond with the documents you have submitted
  • You have to provide the plan for the settlement
  • Evidence of funds is also crucial
  • You have to secure at least sixty points from a hundred in the MPNP EOI Grid for Points
  • If required you have to provide the Job Offer Letter; or You have to demonstrate that your occupation is included in the MPNP List for In-demand Occupations
  • You have to score the least score on the test for language proficiency

What is the MPNP Business Investor Stream?

The earlier MPNP Business Immigration Category has now been replaced by the Business Investor Stream. It permits the MPNP to select eligible investors and entrepreneurs. They have to demonstrate the eligibility and intention for starting or buying a business in Manitoba.

MPNP Business Investor Stream has 2 sub-categories under it and these are:
  • The Farm Investor Pathway
  • The Entrepreneur Pathway

What is the list of In-demand Occupations for Manitoba in 2023?

The list of In-demand Occupations for Manitoba in 2023 consists of 9 categories and these include:

  • Administration, Finance, and Business Occupations
  • Applied and Natural Sciences and linked Occupations
  • Occupations in Healthcare
  • Religion, Government Service, Education, and Social Science Occupations
  • Sport, Recreation, Culture, and Art Occupations
  • Service and Sales Occupations
  • Occupations in Equipment Operators, Transport, Trades, and linked Occupations
  • Occupations exclusive to Primary Industry
  • Occupations exclusive to Utilities, Manufacturing, and Processing

A few rural occupations in demand are also present. But the job offer has to be from the exterior of the Capital Region of Manitoba.

Manitoba PNP Application Processing Time

For the majority of the applications, MPNP takes roughly 4 months. However, the average application processing time is nearly Twelve to Fifteen months.
