Saskatchewan PNP: Why Immigrate to Canada in 2023

Saskatchewan is among the highly diverse provinces of Canada. The scenic natural beauty and aesthetic appeal amplify its attraction. Furthermore, the multicultural society and thriving economy assure a forthcoming ambiance to the immigrants.

What is the Saskatchewan PNP Program?

The Saskatchewan PNP is among the Provincial Nominee Programs of Canada. It enables the province to select skilled overseas workers. The selection is done through the Saskatchewan PNP draw and depends on the economic and labor market demands of the province.

Overseas workers can opt for Saskatchewan SINP Program for immigrate to Canada. The Saskatchewan province welcomed 13,364 immigrants in the period 2019 -2022.

Immigrating to Saskatchewan will be a good choice for the following reasons:

  • High-quality lifestyle
  • Affordable living costs
  • The average salary offered to workers and professionals is really good
  • The housing market is on average quite fair
  • Abundant job opportunities
  • Rapidly expanding provincial economy

Documents required for the Saskatchewan PNP

The documents essential for the Saskatchewan Immigrant Nominee Program application vary based on the selected immigration category.

1. Lawful passport

2. Identity proof

3. Results from Medical tests

4. Evidence of funds

6. Proof of character - Police Clearance Certificate

8. Test results for Language proficiency

9. Plan for Settlement

10. Evidence of previous work experience

11. Certificate of Marriage, if your spouse is accompanying you

12. Information Certificate for the child if your dependent kid is accompanying you

How to Apply for PR in Saskatchewan Immigrant Nominee Program?

The process to apply for Canada PR Visa under SINP without having a job offer is as follows:

  • Stage 1: Create a profile in Express Entry
  • Stage 2: Select Saskatchewan when asked about your preferred location for residence
  • Stage 3: Create your online profile in SINP portal
  • Stage 4: After you complete all the application formalities, the immigration authorities of Saskatchewan will scrutinize the application
  • Stage 5: You will then be offered a timeline for uploading PDF format of your documents
  • Stage 6: Your application for provincial nomination will be approved if your application is considered suitable
  • Stage 7: File application for Canada PR Visa with IRCC
  • Stage 8: You can then receive PR Visa after positive scrutiny of your application

Now, you are ready to land in Canada!

What are the chief immigration streams under Saskatchewan PNP?

The main immigration pathways under the SINP - Saskatchewan immigration nominee program (SINP) are:
  • Overseas Skilled Worker
  • Saskatchewan Experience
  • Farm and Entrepreneur
The immigration sub-categories under the Overseas Skilled Worker stream are as follows:
  • Offer of Employment
  • Express Entry Saskatchewan
  • In-Demand Occupation
The immigration sub-categories under the stream Experience Saskatchewan are as follows:
  • Existing Work Permit Skilled Worker
  • Existing Work Permit Agriculture Worker Semi-Skilled
  • Truck Driver Long-Haul
  • Students
  • Professionals in Healthcare
  • Project Hospitality Sector
The immigration sub-categories under the stream Farm and Entrepreneur are as follows
  • Overseas Graduate Entrepreneur Category
  • Entrepreneur
  • Farm Operator and Owner
  • Youthful Farmers

What is the Saskatchewan PNP Points Assessment Grid?

  • The SINP Assessment Grid for points offers scores to applicants
  • It is applicable for specific immigration pathways
  • The grid has diverse factors for selection
  • A total of 100 points are available
  • The least score needed is 60 points
  • The score is offered to candidates who have submitted EOI
  • The candidates with the highest scores are offered the invitation
  • If candidates score equal points then the candidate having a connection to Saskatchewan is chosen

What are the selection factors under Points Assessment Grid of the SINP?

Maximum Points Selection factors
20 Proficiency in Language
12 Age
23 Training and Educational Qualification
15 Work experience
30 Connection to the labor market in Saskatchewan

What is Saskatchewan PNP’s the Eligibility Criteria?

The eligibility criteria for SINP vary based on the specific immigration category and the sub-category.

Eligibility criteria for the sub-category Occupation In-Demand

The eligibility criteria for the sub-category SINP Occupation In-Demand are

  • You have to secure a minimum of 60 points under the SINP assessment grid for points
  • You need to have completed education at the post-secondary level Or Training Or Apprenticeship
  • You must possess score of at least 4 CLB
  • You must have obtained a Diploma or a Degree or a Certificate
  • You have to apply for a licensure in Saskatchewan of you have underwent training for a job
  • You must possess past work experience
  • You must have obtained work experience in an highly skilled occupation
  • The occupation must not be present on the list of excluded occupations
  • It must be for a minimum of 1 year in the past 10 years
  • The work experience must have been obtained in a full-time job
  • The experience has to be applicable for your education and occupation intended
  • It has to be in a occupation on-demand in NOC B, A, or 0
  • Evidence of funds for settlement
  • Plan for settlement
