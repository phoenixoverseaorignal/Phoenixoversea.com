How to apply for a visit visa from India in 2023?

In recent times, globalization has brought the whole world closer than ever. It seems as if the whole world is a small village. It has opened the gateway to explore beautiful countries while offering multiple life-changing business and career opportunities. Though it has now become easier to visit any country, at the same time there are several visitor visa requirements to fulfil before applying for a visitor visa from India in 2023.

Before moving ahead we would like to clear one misconception about visitor and tourist visas. People generally tend to consider both as the same but this is not true. There is a slight difference between a visitor visa and a tourist visa .

A tourist visa is usually issued for sightseeing. A visit visa, on the other hand, refers to a visa that is granted for a variety of reasons, such as short-term business meetings, medical treatments, and so on.

Tourism is an essential component of global economic growth. However, most individuals travelling to other nations must apply for a tourist visa.

What is a Tourist Visa?

Tourist visas are official travel permits that are provided to foreign tourists for the purpose of leisure travel. For example, if you are from India and wish to visit the United States, you must get a tourist visa. Every nation administers its tourist visa policy, and its respective embassies and consulates will assist you in obtaining a visa for short-term stays. While visiting a foreign nation on a tourist visa, you must follow some rules.

You should keep in mind that it does not allow you to work, business, study or become a permanent resident and so on. Also, there is normally no limit to the number of times you can apply for a tourist visa as long as you match the requirements and have followed the visa rules during your last visit to a particular country .

How to apply for a Tourist Visa?

You should follow these steps to apply for a tourist visa:

Find an embassy or consulate

You must locate a nearby embassy or consulate of your destination country that may issue visas. Here, you must be aware of the fact that an embassy and a consulate are not the same things. They have different roles to play during the whole application process. In any case, after you've located the relevant visa office, you must schedule an appointment to submit your application.

Gather the necessary documents

You must prepare your documents to submit a successful tourist visa application. If your documents are exclusively in your original language, you must translate them as directed by the embassy. Furthermore, some civil papers, such as birth certificates and marriage certificates, may require an apostille stamp.

Pay the necessary tourist visa cost

To get your application completed, you must pay the requisite visa cost, which varies according to your destination. Some embassies request payment before the visa interview, while others may need payment on the same day.

Get ready for the visa interview

You can attend the interview when you have completed your application documentation. You will be questioned about your journey, the reason for your visit, your plans, and how long you want to stay in the nation. This interview is important from the embassy point of view to have a thorough understanding of your background, intentions and the profile.

What is the validity of a Tourist Visa?

It varies according to your desired destination country. A tourist visa is usually issued for a week, 15 days or may be for 30 days or more, depending upon the specific profile and situation. However, some countries may issue a tourist visa for a year with multiple entriesâ still the validity of a tourist visa varies with each country.

What is the processing time of Tourist Visa?

Tourist visa applications are generally processed within a time period of 15 days or 2 weeks of submission. However, the processing period may be less than or greater than two weeks, depending on your place of residency, the season, and the embassy's workload.

Can a tourist visa be Extended?

Most tourist visas cannot be extended unless one of the following conditions is met:

  • You've got an unexpected family problem (birth, illness, death, etc.).
  • Your nation is in a complicated political situation, making it risky to return.
  • Storms, earthquakes, tsunamis, and other natural disasters have caused an unforeseen challenge.

What are the possible reasons for the denial of a tourist visa ?

Your tourist visa application might be turned down for any of the following reasons

  • Misrepresentation of facts and information on your visa application
  • Submission of expired or outdated passport
  • Submission of incomplete application
  • Found guilty of a criminal offence
  • Being previously deported from your destination country for breaking visa rules
